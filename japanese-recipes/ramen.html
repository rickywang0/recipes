Ramen

Description

Ramen is a popular dish that originated from China and was adapted by Japan. It is a soup of noodles and broth, flavored with various seasonings and toppings. Ramen can be made with different types of noodles, broth, and toppings, depending on the region and preference of the cook, which is why there are so many variations of ramen. The recipe here will cover a very popular variation of ramen, tonkotsu ramen.

Tonkotsu ramen is a type of ramen that originated from Fukuoka, Japan. It is made with a rich and creamy broth that is cooked from pork bones for several hours, giving it a milky and collagen-filled texture. The broth is seasoned with soy sauce, salt, miso, or other ingredients, and served with thin and straight noodles. The ramen is usually topped with sliced pork belly, soft-boiled eggs, green onions, and other toppings that add flavor and contrast to the soup. Although Japanese ramen is a rather complex dish and sometimes requires years of training for even the best of Japanese chefs to get right, we've prepared a tonkotsu ramen recipe outlined below.

Ingredients

Here is a recipe for tonkotsu ramen:

  • For the broth:
    • 2 kg of pork bones (trotters or hocks)
    • 500 g of chicken bones (wings or carcasses)
    • 1 onion, peeled and halved
    • 6 cloves of garlic, peeled
    • A thumb-sized piece of ginger, peeled and sliced
    • 1 leek, cut into large pieces
    • A bunch of green onions, cut into large pieces
    • A handful of dried shiitake mushrooms
    • Water
  • For the noodles:
    • 300 g of ramen noodles (fresh or dried)
  • For the toppings:
    • 400 g of pork belly
    • Salt and pepper
    • 4 eggs
    • 4 green onions, thinly sliced
    • A handful of bean sprouts, blanched
    • Sesame seeds, toasted
  • For the seasonings:
    • 1/4 cup of soy sauce
    • 2 tablespoons of fish sauce
    • 2 tablespoons of sugar
    • 2 tablespoons of vinegar
    • 2 tablespoons of mirin
    • 2 tablespoons of sake
    • 4 tablespoons of garlic oil
    • Chili oil, to taste

Steps

  1. To make the broth, rinse the pork bones and chicken bones under cold water and place them in a large pot. Cover with water and bring to a boil. Skim off any scum that rises to the surface. Reduce the heat and simmer for about an hour, skimming occasionally.
  2. Add the onion, garlic, ginger, leek, green onions, and shiitake mushrooms to the pot. Simmer for another hour, or until the broth is rich and creamy. You may need to add more water as it evaporates.
  3. Strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve and discard the solids. Transfer the broth to a clean pot and keep warm over low heat. You should have about 8 cups of broth.
  4. To make the chashu (sliced pork), season the pork belly with salt and pepper and roll it up tightly. Tie it with kitchen twine and place it in a baking dish. Add enough water to cover half of the pork and cover with foil. Bake in a preheated oven at 180Â°C for about an hour and a half, or until tender.
  5. Remove the pork from the baking dish and let it rest for about 10 minutes. Slice thinly and set aside.
  6. To make the soft-boiled eggs, bring a pot of water to a boil and carefully lower the eggs into it. Cook for about six minutes, then transfer to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process. Peel the eggs and cut them in half.
  7. To make the seasonings, combine the soy sauce, fish sauce, sugar, vinegar, mirin, sake, garlic oil, and chili oil in a small saucepan. Bring to a boil and simmer until slightly thickened.
  8. To cook the noodles, bring a large pot of water to a boil and add the noodles. Cook according to the package directions, then drain and rinse under cold water.
  9. To assemble the ramen bowls, divide the noodles among four large bowls. Add some seasonings to each bowl and ladle some hot broth over them. Mix well to dissolve the seasonings. Top with chashu slices, egg halves, green onions, bean sprouts, sesame seeds, and more chili oil if desired.

Good job! You've successfully made tonkotsu ramen. Enjoy your well-deserved meal with lots of slurping!
