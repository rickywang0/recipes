Oyakodon

Description

Oyakodon is a traditional Japanese dish that consists of chicken and egg cooked together in a savory broth and served over rice. The name means “parent and child” rice bowl, because it uses both chicken and egg. It is a simple, comforting, and delicious meal that can be made in less than 30 minutes. There are different variations of oyakodon, such as using beef or pork instead of chicken, or adding salmon and salmon roe for a seafood version. We'll cover the traditional version of oyakodon.

Ingredients

To make oyakodon, you will need the following ingredients:

  • Rice
  • Chicken thighs or breast, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • Eggs, lightly beaten
  • Dashi stock (or water and dashi powder)
  • Soy sauce
  • Mirin (or sugar and sake)
  • Sugar
  • Spring onion, sliced (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the rice according to the package directions or use a rice cooker.
  2. In a small saucepan, bring the dashi stock, soy sauce, mirin, and sugar to a boil over medium-high heat. Adjust the seasoning to your taste.
  3. Add the chicken pieces and simmer until cooked through, about 10 to 15 minutes.
  4. Lower the heat and pour the eggs over the chicken in a thin stream. Cover and cook until the eggs are set to your liking, about 2 to 5 minutes.
  5. Spoon the chicken and egg mixture over the rice and garnish with spring onion if desired.

Oyakodon is very much a Japanese comfort food. Please enjoy this heartwarming dish.
